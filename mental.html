Raising awareness on mental health

Mental health is a topic that needs more awareness. It is not something that people talk about openly and it is considered to be a taboo subject. Mental health problems are not just an individual's problem, but they are also the society's problem. The stigma around mental health has been slowly fading away, but there are still many misconceptions and stereotypes surrounding mental illness.
One of the most common misconceptions is that people with mental illnesses are violent or dangerous. This couldn't be further from the truth. In fact, people with mental illnesses are more likely to be victims of violence than perpetrators of violence.

What is Mental-health

Mental health is the state of someone's mental well-being, including their level of happiness, self-esteem and sense of purpose in life. Mental illness is a diagnosable mental or behavioral disorder that causes significant distress or impairment in functioning.

What is Mental-illness

Mental illnesses are usually diagnosed by a psychiatrist or other qualified healthcare professional. There are many different types of mental illnesses, some are more serious than others. The most common mental illnesses include depression, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) and schizophrenia.





What is Depression

Depression is a mental illness that causes feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and worthlessness. It can be caused by a number of factors such as genetics, childhood trauma, and other diseases. One in five adults in the United States will experience depression at some point in their lives. The most common symptoms are feelings of sadness and worthlessness. There are three types of depression: major depressive disorder (MDD), persistent depressive disorder (PDD), and bipolar disorder (BD). Major depressive disorder is also called clinical depression or unipolar depression. Persistent depressive disorder is also called dysthymia or chronic depression. Bipolar disorder causes highs (mania) and lows (depression).




What is Anxiety

Anxiety is an emotion characterized by feelings of worry, unease, and generalized discomfort. It can be either a short-term "state" or a long-term "trait". The disorder has been divided into six subtypes: generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), and specific phobia.
